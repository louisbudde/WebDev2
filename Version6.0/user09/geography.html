Geography Of The Island

The first image on the left is the location of the Fakarava Atoll from a view from space. As you can see it is pretty far away from any big landmasses. It is about halfway in between the United States and Australia. It is 280 miles north-east of the main island, Tafiti. It has the coordinates of 16.31° S and 145.62° W. Technically, Fakarava isn't actually an island, it's and atoll. An atoll is defined as a ring-shaped island or chain of islands, formed by coral. This "ring" is more of a rectangle, (the image on the right) made of four sides, and two sets of fairly congruent sides. It has an opening of both of the sides, both known to divers around the world as beautiful spots to visit and explore. It is a very beautiful place to explore ith all its wildlife.
It is an atoll in the west of the Tuamotu group in French Polynesia. It is the second largest of the Tuamotu atolls. The nearest land is Toau Atoll, which lies 8.7 miles to the northwest. The opening in the north side, the Pass Guraue, is the largest in all French Polynesia. It has one main villiage, called Rotoava, which is where the majority of the 837 permanent residents stay. It became a part of the UNESCO as a biosphere reserve. This basically means that the U.N. and the residents of the area protect the nature and wildlife from harm as much as they can. In conclusion, the Fakarava Atoll is a very beautiful place with many pretty sights, amazing wildlife, and a perfect place for a nice, relaxing vacations.
